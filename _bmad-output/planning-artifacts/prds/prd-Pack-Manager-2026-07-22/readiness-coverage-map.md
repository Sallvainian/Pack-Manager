# Pack-Manager Normative P0 Readiness Coverage Map

## Purpose and rules

This document is the normative one-to-one companion required by `prd.md`
§3.2. It freezes the legacy P0 denominator at exactly 72 criteria and maps
each criterion to its source consequence, unchanged P0 priority, planning
baseline, provisional closure owner, primary PRD requirement, and closure
batch.

The baseline statuses come from
`_bmad-output/test-artifacts/traceability-matrix.md`. The lane and batch
assignments come from
`_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/prds/prd-Pack-Manager-2026-07-22/extract-gap-evidence.md`
and `_bmad-output/test-artifacts/test-design-progress.md`.

### Revision 2 course-correction interpretation

The 2026-07-24 finalized UX contract changes the required behavior of several
legacy criteria without changing their IDs, P0 priority, or membership in the
72-row denominator. The displayed statuses and 1/52/5 lane split below are
preserved as **revision-1 historical baseline data**, not current credit.

Before this map can be approved or used to claim closure:

1. the Product Behavior Prerequisite UX-PB.1 through UX-PB.5 must reconcile the
   implementation with Decisions D27-D30;
2. every affected row must receive a new behavior-present classification;
3. superseded evidence, especially `AUT-003`, must receive no positive credit;
4. lane and batch totals must be recomputed from that classification; and
5. Product and QA must approve the resulting map revision.

This revision therefore records the new consequences while deliberately making
no readiness promotion, demotion, or candidate claim.

Interpretation rules:

- The provisional `Product/source correction`, `Test infrastructure/coverage`,
  and `Release evidence` lanes apply only to the 58 non-FULL rows. The 14
  baseline-FULL rows are deliberately not forced into the 1/52/5 open-row
  split and have no closure batch.
- Baseline FULL is not final-candidate attestation. Every baseline-FULL row
  must be revalidated under PRD §3.2 and RE-10; it may not be carried forward
  automatically.
- `BP` marks one of the 24 provisionally test-only rows. TIR-1 requires a
  behavior-present check before test authoring. If the behavior is absent or
  incorrect, the row moves to `Product/source correction`; its legacy ID,
  priority, baseline status, and denominator membership do not change.
- Any BP reclassification increments `map_revision`, updates lane/batch totals
  and secondary overlaps, reruns the mechanical validation summary, and
  requires Product and QA reapproval. The prior revision remains available in
  source control and any active Evidence Set names the frozen map revision and
  file digest.
- A primary lane assigns the initial decisive owner; it does not deny a
  secondary product, infrastructure, or candidate-evidence dependency.
- Batch numbers apply only to closure of the 58 non-FULL rows and preserve the
  eight-batch test-design plan. Evidence provenance and final status are still
  governed by TIR-8 and RE-10.
